About Me

I am a Principle Investigator at the Advanced Insitute of Information Technology and a computational biologist at the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard. I have developed computational and statistical methods for many genomics projects. I was advised by Dr. Daniel MacArthur and Dr. Heidi Reim in mendelian genomics, Dr. Ayellet Segre in statistical genetics, Dr. Gad Getz in cancer genomics, Dr. Christina Cuomo in infectious fungal diseases, and Dr. Patrick Schnable in maize genomics. I got my M.S. degree in genetics from Iowa State Univesity, advised by maize geneticst Dr. Patrick Schnable and statistician Dr. Dan Nettleton. Before that, I got my B.S. degree in biochemistry and molecular biology from China Agricultural University.

Research

I aspire to transform human healthcare via scientific, technological and entrepreneurial innovations. My research interests mainly reside in translating state-of-the-art computational technologies into the healthcare space. Currently there are three areas of focus:
1) privacy preserved health information exchange.
2) application of artificial intelligence in medical diagnosis and medical devices.
3) computational biology.
You can learn more about my research from my lab's website.
I am the founder of The Prometheus Health Technologies, Inc., a company using on blockchain technologies to facilitate cancer clinical trials.
